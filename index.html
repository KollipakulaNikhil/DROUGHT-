Causes of Drought

Droughts are caused by a variety of factors including prolonged periods of low rainfall, high temperatures, and changes in weather patterns. Human activities such as deforestation and overuse of water resources can also contribute to drought conditions.

  • Climate Change
  • Deforestation
  • Overuse of Water Resources
  • Changes in Weather Patterns

Effects of Drought

Droughts can have severe impacts on the environment, economy, and society. They can lead to water shortages, crop failures, and increased risk of wildfires. The lack of water can also affect human health and lead to conflicts over water resources.

  • Water Shortages
  • Crop Failures
  • Increased Risk of Wildfires
  • Health Issues
  • Conflicts Over Water Resources

Prevention of Drought

Preventing droughts involves a combination of sustainable water management practices, reforestation, and efforts to combat climate change. Communities can also implement water conservation measures and develop drought-resistant agricultural practices.

  • Sustainable Water Management
  • Reforestation
  • Combating Climate Change
  • Water Conservation Measures
  • Drought-Resistant Agriculture
